PoseNet is a computer vision model and technology that allows for real-time pose estimation from images or video input. It is widely used for applications such as gesture recognition, body tracking, augmented reality, and interactive experiences. Developed by Google's Creative Lab, PoseNet is based on deep learning algorithms and utilizes a convolutional neural network (CNN) to detect and track human body poses. Computer vision is a field of artificial intelligence that focuses on enabling computers to understand and interpret visual information from images or video. It involves the development of algorithms and models that can extract meaningful insights, detect objects or patterns, and perform tasks such as image recognition, object tracking, and pose estimation. Computer vision has applications in various domains, including autonomous vehicles, surveillance systems, medical imaging, robotics, and augmented reality. It combines principles from computer science, mathematics, and machine learning to enable machines to perceive and understand the visual world.
